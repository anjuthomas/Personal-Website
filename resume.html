Speech Synthesis (2011) ~ Created a sound based application in BYOB that converts text to speech. Was chosen as one of the top 5 projects in a class of over 200 students to present as part of the CS-Education day hosted at Berkeley. The project allows people to hear the sounds associated with each letter, words, or sentences without using an online application. This tool can assist the visually impaired, those with reading disabilities, or can help learn the pronunciation of unfamiliar words. Though the project was not able to be fully perfected or encompass all exceptions in the English language due to time restraints, it performs a reasonably good job as a text to speech translator.
